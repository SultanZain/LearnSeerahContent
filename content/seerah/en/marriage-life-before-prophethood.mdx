## Marriage to Khadijah (RA) and Family Life Before Prophethood

### Background of Khadijah (RA)

Khadijah bint Khuwaylid (RA) was among the most respected women of Mecca. She belonged to a noble family of the Quraysh and was widely recognized for her **wisdom, dignity, and integrity**. In a society where trade determined social standing, she was a successful and independent **merchant**, managing caravans that traveled to Syria and Yemen.

Because of her moral excellence, she was known as _At-Tahirah_ (the Pure). Her reputation for honesty and fairness placed her in a position of high esteem within Meccan society.

### The Trust That Led to Marriage

Before their marriage, Khadijah (RA) employed **Muhammad** (PBUH) to lead one of her trade caravans to Syria. His honesty in transactions, fairness with people, and dignified conduct deeply impressed her. Reports from her servant, Maysarah, further highlighted Muhammad’s (PBUH) exceptional character.

Through a trusted intermediary, Khadijah (RA) expressed her wish to marry him. After consultation with family elders, the proposal was accepted.

At the time of marriage, Muhammad (PBUH) was **25 years old**, while Khadijah (RA) was **40 years old**. Their marriage was dignified, simple, and founded upon mutual respect and trust.

### Life Together Before Prophethood

Their marriage marked a period of **stability and emotional security** in the Prophet’s (PBUH) life. Khadijah (RA) provided constant encouragement and reassurance, while Prophet (PBUH) continued his involvement in trade and remained distant from the immoral practices common in Meccan society.

Their household was known for **tranquility, generosity, and ethical conduct**. During this time, the Prophet (PBUH) developed a growing concern for justice, honesty, and social responsibility—qualities that later shaped his prophetic mission.

### Children and Family Environment

The Prophet (PBUH) and Khadijah (RA) were blessed with six children. Classical biographical sources provide the following commonly accepted details:

- **Qasim**  
  Born around **598 CE**. He passed away in early childhood, after which the Prophet (PBUH) came to be known as _Abu al-Qasim_.

- **Zaynab (RA)**  
  Born around **599 CE**. She passed away in **629 CE** (8 AH) in Madinah.

- **Ruqayyah (RA)**  
  Born around **601 CE**. She passed away in **624 CE** (2 AH), during the Battle of Badr.

- **Umm Kulthum (RA)**  
  Born around **603 CE**. She passed away in **630 CE** (9 AH).

- **Fatimah (RA)**  
  Born around **605 CE**. She passed away in **632 CE**, approximately six months after the Prophet’s (PBUH) passing.

- **Abdullah** (also known as _At-Tayyib_ or _At-Tahir_)  
  Born after the beginning of marriage and passed away in early childhood in Mecca.

Their family life emphasized **mercy, care for relatives, patience in hardship, and kindness to others**, forming a nurturing moral environment.

### Significance of This Marriage

This marriage played a vital role in preparing the Prophet (PBUH) for the responsibility of prophethood. Khadijah’s (RA) emotional strength, wisdom, and unwavering trust became a foundation of support during the earliest and most challenging moments of revelation.

Their union remains a lasting example of a relationship built upon **trust, loyalty, and shared moral purpose**, long before the formal beginning of Islam.
