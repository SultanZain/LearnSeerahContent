## Welcome to Section 4: The Makkan Period

This section covers the first thirteen years of the prophetic mission in Mecca. We explore the transition from private conviction to public invitation and the severe opposition faced by the early Muslim community.

### What You Will Learn
* **The Metaphysical Rupture (The First Revelation)**: The profound psychological and physiological weight of the first encounter with Jibril (Gabriel) in the Cave of Hira, and the strategic counseling of Khadijah and Waraqah ibn Nawfal.
* **The Underground Community (Dar al-Arqam)**: The methodology of the secret phase of the Da'wah, analyzing how the Prophet ﷺ established an egalitarian sanctuary to intellectually and spiritually fortify the first generation of Muslims.
* **The Public Proclamation & Tribal Fractures**: The ascent onto Mount Safa and the immediate declaration of the message, detailing how the foundational claim of *Tawhid* fundamentally threatened the economic, political, and theological dominance of the Qurayshi elite.
* **The Anatomy of Resistance**: The tactical evolution of Meccan opposition, shifting from psychological warfare, mockery, and targeted torture of the vulnerable (such as Bilal ibn Rabah and the family of Yasir) to systemic diplomatic and economic boycotts in the Valley of Abu Talib.
* **The Year of Sorrow & The Horizon of Ta’if**: The devastating loss of his familial bulwark (Abu Talib) and emotional anchor (Khadijah), followed by his rejection at Ta'if, illustrating the absolute nadir of his earthly security.

### How to Approach This Section
Observe the structural nature of the early Islamic community. As you analyze the heavy trials of the Makkan period, focus on the pedagogical development of the Sahabah. Their suffering was not arbitrary; it was a foundational training process designed to purge all worldly dependencies and replace them with absolute spiritual conviction.
