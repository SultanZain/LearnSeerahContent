Before examining the specific historical events of the life of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, it is essential to understand the framework of Islamic theology. Revelation did not begin in 7th-century Arabia; it is the culmination of a single message sent to humanity since the dawn of creation.

### What You Will Learn
* **The Primordial Religion**: Evidence that Allah never ordained any religion other than complete submission to Him (Islam), and that all prophets shared this exact theological core.
* **The Prophetic Chain**: The succession of messengers from Adam, through Ibrahim (Abraham), Ismail (Ishmael), Musa (Moses), and Isa (Jesus), establishing a shared legacy.
* **The Finality of Prophethood**: The critical prerequisite belief that Prophet Muhammad ﷺ is the final messenger, closing the door to any subsequent divine revelation.

### How to Approach This Section
Approach these lessons as structural prerequisites. Understanding the continuity of Islam across human history clarifies *why* the final revelation was sent to Mecca and establishes the foundational context needed before exploring the Seerah.
