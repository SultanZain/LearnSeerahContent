## Welcome to Section 5: The Migration (Hijrah)

This section details the critical turning point where the calling transitions from a persecuted minority to an independent community. We look at the strategic planning behind the migration to Medina.

### What You Will Learn
* **Al-Isra’ wal-Mi’raj**: The Night Journey and Ascension to heaven, establishing the daily prayers.
* **The Geopolitical Search (Pledges of Aqabah)**: The tactical shifting of focus toward external tribes during the Hajj seasons, analyzing the First and Second Pledges of Aqabah as the formal legal and military covenants that laid the groundwork for a secure haven.
* **The Hijrah (The Great Migration)**: The masterful blend of absolute divine reliance (*Tawakkul*) and meticulous human planning during the escape from Mecca, detailing the roles of Abu Bakr as-Siddiq, Ali ibn Abi Talib, and the stay in the Cave of Thawr.
* **The Architecture of a Society**: The immediate socio-political reforms implemented upon arrival in Yathrib (now Medina): the construction of the Prophet’s Mosque, the revolutionary binding of brotherhood (*Mu'akhat*) between the Muhajirun and the Ansar, and the drafting of the Constitution of Medina.

### How to Approach This Section
Analyze this phase through the lens of strategic governance and institutionalization. Notice how the message transitions from an individualized, internal spiritual struggle in Mecca to a collective, systemic, and legally codified community structure in Medina.
