**Details of the Event:**

The **Battle of Banu Qaynuqa** (Ghazwat Bani Qaynuqa) was the first major conflict between the Muslims and a Jewish tribe in Medina. Most historians, including Ibn Hajar al-Asqalani, agree that it occurred in the middle of Shawwal, shortly after the victory at Badr.

**The Context of the Expulsion:**
The Banu Qaynuqa were a tribe of skilled goldsmiths who resided in their own fortress within Medina. They were the first among the Jewish tribes to violate the Covenant of Medina after the Battle of Badr by displaying open hostility and provocative behavior toward the Muslims.

**Authentic Evidence:**
The expulsion of the Jewish tribes is documented in the *Sahihayn* (Bukhari and Muslim). Ibn Umar (may Allah be pleased with him) narrated:
> <span className="font-quran" dir="rtl">"حاربَتِ النَّضيرُ وقُرَيْظةُ، فأَجْلى بني النَّضيرِ، وأَقَرَّ قُرَيْظَةَ ومَن عليهم... وأَجْلى يَهودَ المدينةِ كُلَّهم: بني قَيْنُقاعٍ -وهُم رَهْطُ عبدِ الله بنِ سَلامٍ- ويَهودَ بني حارِثةَ، وكُلَّ يَهودِ المدينةِ"</span>
>
> "Banu al-Nadir and Banu Qurayza fought [against the Prophet ﷺ], so he expelled Banu al-Nadir and allowed Banu Qurayza to stay... and he expelled all the Jews of Medina: Banu Qaynuqa—who were the kinsmen of Abdullah ibn Salam—and the Jews of Banu Haritha, and all the Jews of Medina."

**Outcome:**
The Prophet ﷺ besieged their fortresses for fifteen days until they surrendered. Initially, they were to be punished for their treachery, but after the intervention of Abdullah ibn Ubayy, the Prophet ﷺ ordered their expulsion from Medina. They departed for Ash-Sham (Greater Syria), leaving behind their weaponry and goldsmithing tools as spoils for the Muslims.
