**Details of the Event:**

The **Battle of as-Sawiq** occurred in Dhu al-Hijjah, 2 AH, approximately two months after the Quraysh's defeat at Badr. Abu Sufyan, seeking to restore his honor, had vowed not to perform ritual purification until he had struck a blow against the Muslims.

**The Infiltration and Intelligence:**
Abu Sufyan led 200 riders from Makkah via the Najdiyyah route, arriving at a mountain called **Naib** near Medina. Under the cover of night, he attempted to meet with Huyay ibn Akhtab of the Banu Nadir, but Huyay refused to open his door out of fear. Abu Sufyan then went to **Salam ibn Mishkam**, the leader and treasurer of Banu Nadir, who hosted him with food and drink and provided vital intelligence regarding the Muslims' activities.

**The Raid on al-Urayd:**
Abu Sufyan’s men launched a quick strike on a valley called **al-Urayd**. They:
* Burned down several palm groves.
* Killed an Ansari man named **Ma'bad ibn 'Amr** and his ally who were working in their fields.

**The Pursuit and the "Porridge":**
When the Prophet ﷺ received the news, he immediately led 200 Muhajirin and Ansar in pursuit, appointing **Abu Lubaba** as his deputy in Medina. Realizing the speed of the Muslim response, the Quraysh fled. To lighten their loads and increase their speed, they discarded their heavy supply bags of **Sawiq** (parched grain porridge).

The Muslims pursued them until **Qarqarat al-Kudr** but were unable to overtake them. They returned to Medina with the discarded supplies, leading the companions to name it the **"Battle of the Sawiq."** When they asked if this was a full expedition, the Prophet ﷺ confirmed: 
> "Yes."
