**Details of the Event:**

Ruqayyah (may Allah be pleased with her) was the daughter of the Prophet ﷺ and the wife of **Uthman ibn Affan** (may Allah be pleased with him). Her life was marked by early sacrifices for the sake of Islam.

**Background:**
* Before the advent of Islam, she was married to **Utbah**, the son of Abu Lahab. When the Prophet ﷺ began his mission, Abu Lahab ordered his son to divorce her in an attempt to harass the Prophet ﷺ.
* Uthman ibn Affan then married her, and together they were among the first to migrate for the sake of Allah to **Abyssinia** (*Al-Habashah*).

**Her Illness and the Battle of Badr:**
When the call was made for the Muslims to intercept the caravan of Abu Sufyan (leading to the Battle of Badr), Ruqayyah was severely ill. The Prophet ﷺ specifically commanded Uthman to stay behind in Medina to nurse her and care for her.

> Because of this command, Uthman did not participate in the actual fighting at Badr. However, the Prophet ﷺ acknowledged his service by granting him a share of the spoils and the reward of the battle as if he had been present.

**Her Passing:**
While the Muslims were at Badr, Ruqayyah's health continued to decline. She passed away in Medina, and her death was a moment of deep sorrow for the Prophetic household. The news of the victory at Badr arrived in Medina shortly after her burial.
