**Details of the Event:**

In the month of **Shawwal, 2 AH** (twenty months after the Hijrah), the Prophet ﷺ sanctioned an expedition led by **Salim ibn Umayr al-Ansari** against **Abu Afak**, a Jewish elder from the Banu Amr ibn Awf. 

**The Incitement of Abu Afak:**
Abu Afak was a 120-year-old man whose heart was filled with animosity toward Islam. Despite his age, he was a vocal opponent who used his poetry to:
* Insult the Messenger of Allah ﷺ.
* Incite the people to hostility against the Muslims.
* Mock the Ansar for their support and protection of the Prophet ﷺ, especially after the Muslims gained strength following the victory at Badr.

**The Vow of Salim ibn Umayr:**
Salim ibn Umayr (may Allah be pleased with him), a veteran of the Battle of Badr and one of the *Bakkā'īn* (the Weepers), was deeply pained by these insults. He made a solemn vow:
> <span className="font-quran" dir="rtl">"عليَّ نَذرٌ أن أقتُلَ أبا عَفَكٍ أو أموتَ دُونَه"</span>
>
> "I take a vow to Allah that I shall either kill Abu Afak or die in the attempt."

**The Execution:**
Salim waited for an opportunity until a hot summer night when Abu Afak slept in an open courtyard. Salim approached him and placed his sword against Abu Afak's liver, leaning on it until it pierced through him. The cry of the enemy of Allah alerted his followers, who buried him the next day.

**Legacy of Salim ibn Umayr:**
Salim ibn Umayr was a devoted companion who participated in all major battles, including Badr, Uhud, and Khandaq. He remained steadfast in his faith until his passing during the caliphate of Muawiyah ibn Abi Sufyan (may Allah be pleased with them).
