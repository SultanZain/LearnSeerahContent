**Details of the Event:**

Asma bint Marwan was a woman from the tribe of Banu Umayyah ibn Zayd and the wife of Yazid ibn Zayd ibn Hisn al-Khatmi. She was known for her open hostility toward Islam, her frequent insults against the Messenger of Allah ﷺ, and her incitement of others against him through her poetry. Furthermore, she would desecrate the mosque of the Banu Khatmah tribe.

**The Vow of Umayr ibn Adi:**
Umayr ibn Adi (may Allah be pleased with him), who was the first from the Banu Khatmah to embrace Islam and was known as "The Qari" (the Reciter), made a solemn vow. He pledged that if the Messenger of Allah ﷺ returned safely from the Battle of Badr to Medina, he would personally put an end to her harmful actions.

**The Fulfillment:**
Upon the Prophet's ﷺ return, Umayr carried out his vow. When he encountered her family burying her, they questioned him: "O Umayr, did you kill her?" He replied with great courage:
> <span className="font-quran" dir="rtl">"نَعَم، فكيدوني جَميعًا ثم لا تُنظِرونِ، فَوَالَّذي نَفسي بيَدِه لو قلتُم بأجمَعِكُم ما قالَت لضَرَبتُكُم بسَيفي هذا حتَّى أموتَ أو أقتُلَكُم"</span>
>
> "Yes. So plot against me, all of you, and do not give me respite! By Him in Whose hand is my soul, if all of you were to say what she said, I would strike you with this sword of mine until I either die or kill you."

**The Impact on Banu Khatmah:**
Following this event, the status of Islam in Banu Khatmah shifted. Those who had been practicing their faith in secret began to practice it openly. This moment marked the public manifestation of Islam within the tribe, largely due to the unwavering conviction of Umayr ibn Adi.
