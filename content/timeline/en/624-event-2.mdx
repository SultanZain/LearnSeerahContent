**Details of the Event:**

In Rajab, 17 months after the Hijrah, the Prophet ﷺ sent **Abdullah ibn Jahsh al-Asadi** with twelve men from the *Muhajirin*. This expedition is noted for several historical "firsts" in Islam.

**The Sealed Command:**
The Prophet ﷺ gave Abdullah a letter and commanded him <u>not to open it</u> until he had traveled for two days. Upon opening it, the instructions were:
> <span className="font-quran" dir="rtl">"إذا نَظَرْتَ في كِتابي هذا، فامْضِ حتى تَنزِلَ نَخلةَ بينَ مكَّةَ والطَّائِفِ فتَرصُدَ بها قُريشًا، وتَعلَمَ لنا من أخبارِهِم"</span>
>
> "When you have looked into this letter of mine, proceed until you reach Nakhlah between Makkah and Taif. Lie in wait there for the Quraysh and bring us news of them."

**The Incident at Nakhlah:**
The group encountered a Quraysh trade caravan. They faced a dilemma: it was the last day of the sacred month of **Rajab**. If they fought, they would violate the sacred month; if they waited, the caravan would reach the safety of the Haram. They chose to attack:
* **Amr ibn al-Hadrami** was killed (the *first* person killed in a conflict in Islam).
* **Two prisoners** were taken (the *first* prisoners in Islam).
* The **Khums** (one-fifth) of the booty was set aside (the *first* Khums in Islam).
