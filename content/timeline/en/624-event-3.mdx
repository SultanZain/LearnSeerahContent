**Details of the Event:**

The changing of the **Qibla** (the direction of prayer) from Jerusalem (*Bait al-Maqdis*) to the **Kaaba** in Makkah is one of the most significant events in early Islamic history.

**Duration and Desire:**
According to Al-Bara' bin 'Azib (may Allah be pleased with him), after arriving in Medina, the Messenger of Allah ﷺ prayed toward Jerusalem for **sixteen or seventeen months**. However, he ﷺ deeply desired to face the Kaaba, the House built by his father Ibrahim (peace be upon him). He would often look toward the heavens, awaiting divine instruction.

**The Revelation:**
In the month of **Sha'ban**, Allah answered his supplication and revealed:

<Quran surah="2" ayah="144" />

**The Response of the Sahaba:**
The news of the change spread rapidly through Medina:
* A man who had prayed the **Asr prayer** with the Prophet ﷺ toward the new Qibla passed by a group of the Ansar.
* They were currently in the middle of their own prayer, specifically in the position of **Ruku'** (bowing).
* When he testified that the Prophet ﷺ had been directed toward the Kaaba, the entire congregation **turned while still in prayer** to face the new direction.

> This event serves as a profound example of the Sahaba's <u>immediate obedience</u> and total submission to the commands of Allah and His Messenger ﷺ.
