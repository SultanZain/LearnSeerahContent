**Details of the Event:**

The **Great Battle of Badr**, also known as *Yawm al-Furqan* (The Day of Criterion), is the most significant military encounter in early Islamic history. It took place on the 17th of Ramadan, marking the first major victory for the Muslims against the Quraysh.

**The Prelude:**
The Prophet ﷺ initially led a group of over **300 Muslims** to intercept a rich trade caravan returning from Sham, led by **Abu Sufyan**. However, Abu Sufyan managed to escape by changing his route and sent word to Makkah for reinforcements. The Quraysh responded by sending an army of nearly **1,000 men**, including their prominent leaders.

**The Battle:**
The two forces met at the wells of Badr. The battle began with three famous duels, followed by a fierce general engagement. Allah supported the believers with an army of angels.
* **Makkan Losses:** 70 killed (including leaders like **Abu Jahl** and **Umayyah ibn Khalaf**) and 70 taken as prisoners.
* **Muslim Martyrs:** 14 men (some reports suggest 16).

**The Aftermath:**
A significant discussion arose regarding the prisoners of war. **Abu Bakr** (may Allah be pleased with him) suggested taking a ransom to help the Muslims, while **Umar ibn al-Khattab** (may Allah be pleased with him) suggested they be executed to break the strength of the polytheists.
