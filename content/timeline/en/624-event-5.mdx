**Details of the Event:**

During the month of **Ramadan** in the second year of the Hijrah, Allah the Almighty ordained **Zakat al-Fitr** (also known as Sadaqat al-Fitr). 

**The Ruling:**
It was made obligatory upon every Muslim, whether free or enslaved, male or female, young or old, provided they possess the means to give it. It is typically paid before the Eid al-Fitr prayer.

**The Wisdom Behind It:**
The Prophet ﷺ established this practice for two primary spiritual and social reasons:
1. **Purification:** To purify the person fasting from any unseemly speech (*laghw*) or obscenity (*rafath*) committed during the month of Ramadan.
2. **Social Support:** To provide for the poor and needy, ensuring they can join in the joy and celebrations of the day of Eid.

This obligation reinforced the communal bond of the early Muslim community in Medina, ensuring that even the most vulnerable members could celebrate the completion of the holy month with dignity.
