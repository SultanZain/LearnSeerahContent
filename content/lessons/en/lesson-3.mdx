## The Question That Follows

In the last lesson, we established that humanity needs Prophets — that the human mind alone cannot determine the full path to Allah, and that justice demands a warning before accountability.

But a sharp mind will immediately ask: *if Allah wanted to guide us, why the intermediary at all? Why a Prophet? Why not simply speak to each of us directly — place the truth in every heart, in every language, at every moment?*

It is a fair question. And it deserves a careful answer.

{/* page */}

## This World Is a Test

The answer begins with understanding what this world actually is.

Allah did not create this life as a place of comfort or finality. It is *Dar al-Ibtila* — the Abode of Trial. Every human being is here to be tested: in their patience, in their gratitude, in their choices when the right path costs something and the wrong path appears easier.

<Quran surah="67" ayah="2" />

Now consider what would happen if Allah spoke to every person directly — not through a Prophet, not through a scripture, but as an unmistakable, undeniable divine voice addressing each soul personally.

There would be no test. There would be no choice. The overwhelming reality of direct divine address would leave no room for the exercise of free will. A person compelled to believe by irresistible divine speech has not chosen faith — he has simply been overpowered by it.

And a faith that is forced is not faith at all. It is surrender without meaning.

This is why the nature of guidance in this world is deliberate. The evidence is real and powerful — the universe, the scripture, the Prophet — but it is evidence that must be *sought*, *weighed*, and *chosen*. That act of seeking and choosing is precisely what the trial is about.

## Why Not an Angel?

A second natural question follows: *fine, not direct speech — but why a human Prophet? Why not an angel, a being of pure light and perfect obedience, untouched by sin?*

The Quraysh of Mecca actually raised this objection. They said: *if this were truly a divine message, surely a great angel would have been sent with it.* Allah answered them directly:

<Quran surah="6" ayah="8-9" />

The answer is profound in its simplicity. If an angel were sent as a Prophet to humanity, he would have to take human form — otherwise no human being could bear to look at him, let alone listen to him and live normally alongside him.

But more importantly: an angel cannot be a model for a human life.

An angel does not know hunger. He has never felt the weight of grief at the loss of a child. He has never experienced the temptation to lie when the truth is costly, or to give up when the road becomes unbearable. His obedience to Allah costs him nothing, because he has no lower self pulling him in the opposite direction.

When an angel worships, it is simply his nature. When a human being — exhausted, grieving, persecuted, afraid — still chooses to worship Allah and remain truthful, that is something else entirely. That is a triumph of will and faith that carries real weight.

A human Prophet who does this is a proof that it *can be done*. He is not just a postman delivering a letter. He is a living demonstration of the message itself.

## The Living Proof

This brings us to one of the most important functions of Prophethood that is often overlooked.

A scripture, on its own, tells you *what* to do. A Prophet shows you *how*.

How do you remain patient when everything is taken from you? Watch Ibrahim <span className="font-ar">عليه السلام</span> — cast into a fire, commanded to leave his infant son in a barren valley, then asked to sacrifice that same son with his own hand — and never once losing his trust in Allah.

How do you lead without arrogance? Watch Musa <span className="font-ar">عليه السلام</span> — raised in Pharaoh's palace, given power and miracles — weeping in humility before Allah, saying: *My Lord, I have wronged myself.*

How do you forgive those who persecuted you for a lifetime? Watch Muhammad ﷺ — standing at the gates of Mecca on the day of its conquest, facing the very people who had tortured his companions, killed his family members, and driven him from his home — and offering them a general amnesty.

<Quran surah="33" ayah="21" />

No scripture alone could have taught these lessons with the same force. You needed to see them lived. That is what a Prophet is — a human being whose life is itself a proof.

## The Wisdom of the Method

So when we ask *why not direct guidance* — the answer is not that Allah is distant or indifferent. The answer is the opposite.

Direct divine speech would have bypassed the human will entirely. An angelic messenger would have been beyond human imitation. A scripture without a living example would have left us guessing at what the words look like in a real life.

The method Allah chose — a human being, chosen and purified, living among his people, suffering what they suffer, yet never wavering — is the most complete form of guidance possible.

It reaches the mind through scripture. It reaches the heart through example. And it leaves the will entirely free.

That is the wisdom of Prophethood. And that is why, when we study the life of Muhammad ﷺ, we are not reading biography for its own sake. We are reading the most complete demonstration of what a human life devoted to Allah actually looks like.

Everything in the Seerah — every hardship, every moment of patience, every act of mercy — is guidance made visible.
