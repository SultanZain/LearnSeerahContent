## Three Years Were Enough

For three years, Islam had grown in the quiet spaces of Mecca — in the house of al-Arqam, in private conversations, in hearts that had heard the message personally and given it their private conviction.

But the message was not sent to be kept private. It was sent to warn all of humanity.

The command came:

<Quran surah="26" ayah="214" />

*And warn your nearest kindred.*

The secret phase was ending. The storm was about to begin.

## The Family Gathering

Before going to the hill of Safa, the Prophet ﷺ followed the divine instruction and invited his own family first.

He arranged a meal for the men of Banu Hashim — approximately forty men. He fed them and rose to speak. Abu Lahab interrupted before he could finish. The Prophet ﷺ arranged a second meal and this time spoke before anyone could stop him:

*"O sons of Abd al-Muttalib — I know of no man in all of Arabia who has brought his people something better than what I bring you. I bring you the good of this world and the next. Who among you will support me in this matter?"*

The room was silent. Then Ali رضي الله عنه — the youngest man present — stood.

*"I will, O Prophet of Allah."*

The men of Banu Hashim looked at each other awkwardly and left. It was not the response the moment deserved. But it was the one it received.

## The Hill of Safa

The Prophet ﷺ climbed the hill of Safa and called out the ancient cry of alarm:

*"Ya Sabahah!"*

The people of Mecca came running — because that cry meant danger, and a community that trusted the man calling it came to find out what he had seen.

They gathered at the foot of the hill, clan by clan. The Prophet ﷺ looked down at them — his people, who had called him Al-Amin, who had trusted him for forty years.

*"If I told you that there was an army behind this mountain, coming to attack you — would you believe me?"*

*"Yes. We have never known you to lie."*

*"Then I am a warner to you before a severe punishment."*

## The First Response

Then Abu Lahab spoke. He did not engage with the message. He did not ask questions. He cursed.

*"Tabbat yadak ya Muhammad! May your hands perish — is this why you gathered us?"*

He turned and walked away. The crowd dispersed.

And Allah responded:

<Quran surah="111" ayah="1-5" />

*May the hands of Abu Lahab perish, and may he perish. His wealth and his gains will not benefit him. He will burn in a fire of blazing flame — and his wife as well, the carrier of firewood, around her neck a rope of twisted fiber.*

The surah condemned Abu Lahab and his wife — who placed thorns in the Prophet's ﷺ path — by name. The honor of the Prophet ﷺ was not his alone to defend.

## What the Hill of Safa Meant

From this day, every person in Mecca knew. The man they had called Al-Amin was standing on a hill and telling them that their entire way of life was wrong. That the idols in the Kaaba were nothing. That the social order was an abomination. That there was one God, one law, one reckoning.

Some heard and were moved toward it. Those people would fill Dar al-Arqam.

Most heard and felt the threat to everything they had built their lives on. Those people would make the next thirteen years the hardest years any Prophet had faced since Nuh <span className="font-ar">عليه السلام</span>.

The hill of Safa was a line. Before it — the secret community, growing in safety. After it — the community in the open, facing Quraysh's full power and fury.

The persecution was coming. And it would arrive with a speed and ferocity that would test every soul that had said yes to the message right down to its roots.
