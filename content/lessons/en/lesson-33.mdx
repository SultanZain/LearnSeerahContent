## Begin Where You Are

The command was: *arise and warn.*

But warn whom first? In what order? With what strategy?

The answer, revealed through divine guidance and the wisdom of a man who understood his community, was: begin close. Begin with those who know you. Begin where the trust already exists.

For three years, the dawah of Islam was quiet, personal, and intimate — conducted in private homes, in quiet conversations, in the spaces between the public life of Mecca where something genuine could be said and heard without the performance that public spaces demanded.

## The First to Say Yes

Khadijah رضي الله عنها had already believed — on the first night, before the mission had even been formally announced. She was the first.

The second was Ali ibn Abi Talib <span className="font-ar">رضي الله عنه</span> — the Prophet's ﷺ young cousin, who had been living in his household since childhood when Abu Talib's difficult financial circumstances had led the Prophet ﷺ to take him in. Ali was perhaps ten years old when the revelation came. He heard the Prophet ﷺ and Khadijah رضي الله عنها praying together one morning and asked what they were doing. The Prophet ﷺ told him. Ali asked for a night to think.

The next morning he came back: *I don't need to consult anyone. Allah created me without consulting my father. So why should I consult my father about worshipping Allah?*

He was ten years old, and he had already understood something that many adults never reach.

The third was Zayd ibn Harithah <span className="font-ar">رضي الله عنه</span> — a man who had been brought to Mecca as a slave as a boy, purchased by Khadijah رضي الله عنها, and given to the Prophet ﷺ as a gift. The Prophet ﷺ had freed him immediately and offered to let him return to his family. Zayd chose to stay. The Prophet ﷺ had taken him to the Kaaba and declared before the people of Quraysh: *Zayd is my son and I am his father.* A man who had been a slave had become, in the sight of all Mecca, a son. When Islam came, Zayd's choice was easy.

The fourth was Abu Bakr ibn Abi Quhafah <span className="font-ar">رضي الله عنه</span> — the closest friend Muhammad ﷺ had ever had, a man of the same age, a merchant of impeccable reputation and broad social connections. The Prophet ﷺ told him. He did not hesitate for a single moment.

## Abu Bakr Goes to Work

From the moment Abu Bakr رضي الله عنه accepted Islam, he did not keep it to himself.

He went to his closest friends. The men whose company he had always kept — merchants of standing, men of intelligence and character, men whose judgment was respected in Mecca. He told them what he had heard and what he believed.

And one by one, they accepted.

Uthman ibn Affan <span className="font-ar">رضي الله عنه</span> — wealthy, noble, gentle. Zubayr ibn al-Awwam <span className="font-ar">رضي الله عنه</span> — the nephew of Khadijah رضي الله عنها, a young man of courage. Abd al-Rahman ibn Awf <span className="font-ar">رضي الله عنه</span> — a merchant of extraordinary commercial skill. Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas <span className="font-ar">رضي الله عنه</span> — young, passionate, from a respected family. Talhah ibn Ubaydillah <span className="font-ar">رضي الله عنه</span> — another merchant of standing and character.

Five of the ten companions promised Jannah accepted Islam through the dawah of Abu Bakr رضي الله عنه in these first days. This is a detail worth sitting with — the effectiveness of personal, relationship-based dawah, conducted by a person whose own character had already established the credibility of everything he said.

The early Muslim community was growing — quietly, one conversation at a time, among people who knew and trusted each other.

## The House of al-Arqam

As the number of believers grew beyond the immediate circle of the Prophet's ﷺ closest companions, a place was needed — somewhere to gather, to learn, to pray together, to build the community in safety.

They found it in the house of al-Arqam ibn Abi al-Arqam <span className="font-ar">رضي الله عنه</span> — a young man from the clan of Makhzum, one of the less prominent clans, whose house was near the Kaaba but sufficiently out of the way to provide some degree of privacy.

Dar al-Arqam — the House of al-Arqam — became the first school, the first mosque, and the first community center of Islam. The Prophet ﷺ taught there. The companions رضي الله عنهم learned there. The Quran was memorized there. Questions were asked and answered. The bonds that would carry the early community through the coming persecution were formed there.

It was not a grand establishment. It was a room in a house, filled with people who had accepted a message that the rest of their city considered madness.

But it was the seed of everything that followed.

## Who Was Accepting

The profile of the early Muslims tells us something important about the nature of the message.

Some were wealthy and respected — like Khadijah رضي الله عنها, Abu Bakr رضي الله عنه, and Uthman رضي الله عنه. Their acceptance could not be explained by material desperation.

Some were young — like Ali رضي الله عنه and Sa'd ibn Abi Waqqas <span className="font-ar">رضي الله عنه</span>, who accepted in their teens. Their acceptance spoke to the clarity and power of the message itself, unclouded by decades of vested interest in the existing order.

And some — increasingly, as the dawah spread — were from the margins of Meccan society. Slaves. The poor. Women with no tribal protection. People for whom the existing order offered nothing, and for whom a message of human dignity and divine accountability was not just true but urgently, personally necessary.

The early Muslim community was not homogeneous. It was the beginning of something that had never existed in the Jahiliyyah — a community defined not by tribe or wealth or lineage, but by a shared belief and a shared Lord.

For three years, this community grew in secret. It grew in houses and on hillsides and in quiet conversations between people who had found something they had not known they were looking for.

Then came the command to go public.

And everything that had been growing in secret was about to face the full weight of the world that did not want it to grow at all.
